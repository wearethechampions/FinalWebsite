Mosquito Press

  Journalists fired up the politicization of the Filipinos, many of whom were women, who risked their lives for freedom and democracy.

  During Martial Law, Mosquito Press was the term coined in journalism. The Philippine Collegian, which continued to criticize the Martial Law government is a publication contained in the Mosquito Press, despite the dangers this entailed. Mosquitoes, small but have a stinging bite, are likened to these publications. During the martial law period the press was highly controlled.
